# Stock Selection

## Contents

- Objective
- Define the mandate
- Validate the direction and economic chain
- Define roles and pools
- Compare hard data and evidence
- Resolve role fit and freeze the selection

## Objective

Produce a defensible selected basket without assigning portfolio weights. Separate market direction, company quality, role fit, and evidence confidence.

## 1. Define The Mandate

Record:

- Investment horizon and benchmark.
- Existing holdings and prohibited overlap.
- Allowed security types, exchanges, ADRs, and OTC lines.
- Desired role count or basket shape, when supplied.
- Whether the task is discovery, validation, replacement, or review.

## 2. Validate The Direction

Start above the single-stock level. Use suitable benchmarks, sector or theme baskets, relative strength, breadth, and fundamental or macro evidence to decide whether the direction deserves research.

Label price momentum as market evidence, not proof of future earnings.

## 3. Map The Economic Chain

Describe the complete chain before narrowing it. Identify:

- Demand source.
- Physical or economic bottlenecks.
- Suppliers and enabling infrastructure.
- Revenue capture points.
- Regulation, financing, capacity, and execution constraints.
- Existing-portfolio overlap.

Then state why the selected sub-chain fits this mandate better than excluded branches.

Build a complete bottleneck tree, positive transmission graph, counter-evidence graph, and node evidence checklist by following [bottleneck-analysis.md](bottleneck-analysis.md). Complete this work before defining roles or individual names.

## 4. Define Roles Before Names

Create portfolio roles that describe distinct economic jobs, not marketing labels. A role must explain:

- Which bottleneck it addresses.
- How it captures value.
- What evidence would validate it.
- Which other roles share the same risk driver.

Do not force a weak company into a role merely to complete a visually balanced chain.

## 5. Build Three Pools

- `research`: names receiving full evidence work.
- `watch`: plausible alternatives with incomplete evidence or weaker fit.
- `comparator`: benchmarks, incumbents, existing holdings, or different business models used only for context.

Pool membership is a research status, not a recommendation.

## 6. Compare Hard Data Within Peer Groups

Declare peer groups before ranking. Prefer fields such as:

- Revenue growth and margin quality.
- Operating cash flow and free cash flow.
- Balance-sheet capacity.
- Valuation measures with explicit denominator and period.
- Historical valuation position when genuinely available.
- Price position, volatility, and drawdown as risk proxies.
- Orders and contracted demand only within compatible definitions.

Show missing fields separately. Do not reward or punish missing values through silent imputation.

## 7. Validate Customers, Orders, And Evidence

Classify evidence:

- `A`: Quantified revenue, sales, orders, or exposure from a primary source.
- `B`: Direct company disclosure without a quantified share.
- `C`: Business-description or industry-link inference.
- `U`: Unverified or conflicting.

Keep customer stability, demand elasticity, concentration risk, and evidence strength separate. Do not turn evidence grades into a universal numeric score.

## 8. Resolve Role Fit And Duplication

For each role, state:

- Selected name and why it fits.
- Best replacement and what would cause a switch.
- Watch names and missing evidence.
- Comparator names and why they are not selections.
- Shared risks with other selected names.

Allow a lower hard-data rank to be selected only when the role-fit decision is explicit and supported. Never rewrite the hard-data ranking to make the final choice look inevitable.

## 9. Freeze The Selection

The selection report must contain:

- Thesis and scope.
- Research chain and basket roles.
- Universe and pool membership.
- Peer-group comparisons.
- Customer, order, and source evidence.
- Selected basket, alternatives, and overlap risks.
- Data gaps, invalidation conditions, and next review date.
- Status: `draft`, `reviewed`, or `frozen`.

Only a human approval may change the status to `frozen`.
